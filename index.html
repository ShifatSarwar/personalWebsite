Camouflage

Computation offloading has become essential for modern applications, but it also poses privacy risks. In this paper, we show that even simple machine learning algorithms can identify offloaded computations. To address this, we propose Camouflage, a framework that conceals computation characteristics to thwart malicious actors. Our evaluation demonstrates that Camouflage reduces identification of computations by up to 60% with minimal overhead. You can find the published paper here.

Automated Requirement Engineering Toolkit

The Automated Requirement Engineering Toolkit leverages cutting-edge machine learning and natural language processing techniques to revolutionize requirement engineering. It automates requirement elicitation, classification, conflict detection, and resolution with three core deep-learning models. DaVinci003 generates requirements from user input, while a custom neural network fetches related requirements. An RNN-LSTM model classifies functional/non-functional requirements. The toolkit excels in conflict detection and resolution, converting statements into elements, applying rules, and offering efficient conflict resolution methods. It ensures coherence and accuracy by prioritizing and adjusting conflicting requirements based on system type.
